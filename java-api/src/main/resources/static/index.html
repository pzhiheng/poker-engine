        📖 How to Play

        1 — Texas Hold'em Basics

        Texas Hold'em is the world's most popular poker game. Each player gets 2 private hole cards and shares 5 community cards. Your goal is to make the best 5-card hand from any combination of your 2 cards and the 5 community cards.

        Hand Rankings (best → worst)
        Royal FlushA K Q J T (same suit)
        Straight Flush5 consecutive same suit
        Four of a KindFour cards of same rank
        Full HouseThree of a kind + a pair
        Flush5 cards of the same suit
        Straight5 consecutive cards
        Three of a KindThree cards of same rank
        Two PairTwo different pairs
        One PairTwo cards of same rank
        High CardHighest single card
        Table Positions
        UTG — Under the Gun (first to act preflop) MP — Middle Position CO — Cutoff (2nd from right) BTN — Button (Dealer, best position) SB — Small Blind (posts SB, acts 2nd preflop) BB — Big Blind (posts BB, last to act preflop)
        2 — The Four Betting Rounds
        PREFLOP
        Hole cards dealt. SB + BB posted. UTG acts first.
        FLOP
        3 community cards revealed. SB acts first.
        TURN
        4th community card. Bets usually increase.
        RIVER
        5th card. Final betting round. Then showdown.
        Your Actions Each Round
        FOLD — give up your hand, lose any chips already bet CHECK — pass the action (only when no bet facing) CALL — match the current bet to stay in BET / RAISE — put more chips in to increase the price ALL-IN — commit all remaining chips
        3 — What is GTO?

        Game Theory Optimal (GTO) means playing in a way that cannot be exploited, even if your opponent knows your strategy perfectly.

        A GTO player mixes between value bets and bluffs in mathematically precise ratios, making their range balanced and unpredictable. No opponent can gain an edge simply by knowing you play GTO.

        Why does GTO matter for beginners?

        You don't need to play perfect GTO to improve. Understanding GTO concepts teaches you the right reasons to make decisions — equity, pot odds, position advantage. This app uses simplified GTO heuristics to give you coaching feedback after every decision.

        ✓ GTO says: Bet strong hands for value
        Build big pots when you have equity advantage
        ✓ GTO says: Fold when equity < pot odds
        Long-run EV is negative when you call without the right price
        ✓ GTO says: Mix bluffs with value bets
        Makes your bets unpredictable — opponents can't just fold to every bet
        ✓ GTO says: Defend your BB appropriately
        Folding too often to steals makes you exploitable
        4 — Pot Odds & Equity (The Math)

        Every call decision comes down to a simple comparison: your equity vs. your pot odds.

        Pot Odds Formula
        Pot Odds = call_amount ÷ (pot + call_amount)
        Example: Pot = 30, call = 10 → Pot Odds = 10 ÷ 40 = 25%
        If your equity (win probability) is above 25%, calling is profitable long-term.
        Counting Outs
        An "out" is a card that would improve your hand. Count your outs then multiply:
        Flop equity ≈ outs × 4%   |   Turn equity ≈ outs × 2%
        Example: Flush draw = 9 outs × 4% = ~36% equity on the flop.
        Position Advantage

        Acting last in a betting round (BTN, CO) is a massive advantage. You see what everyone does before you decide. Position can be worth 10-15% extra equity in practice. Play tighter OOP (out of position), wider IP (in position).
